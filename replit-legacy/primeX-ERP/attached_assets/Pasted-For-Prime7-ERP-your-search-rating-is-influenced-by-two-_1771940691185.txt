For Prime7 ERP, your search rating is influenced by two major factors: the highly competitive nature of the "ERP software" market and the technical "health" of your specific domain.
Based on a high-level check of your site's current standing, here are the most effective ways to increase your search rating:
1. Differentiate from "Tally Prime"
A major challenge for your domain is that search engines often confuse "Prime 7" with "Tally Prime 7.0" (a very popular accounting software). To fix this:
 * Update Title Tags: Ensure your homepage title isn't just "Prime7 ERP." Use: “Prime7 ERP | Customizable Enterprise Resource Planning for [Your Specific Industry]”.
 * Unique Value Proposition: Clearly state on your homepage how you differ from Tally. If you specialize in manufacturing, distribution, or a specific region, use those keywords prominently.
2. Fix Technical "Trust" Signals
ERP software deals with sensitive financial data. Search engines like Google will not rank an ERP site highly if it doesn't look secure.
 * Security Headers: Ensure your SSL (HTTPS) is not just present but configured correctly.
 * Contact Clarity: Google’s "Trust" algorithm (E-E-A-T) looks for a physical address, a working phone number, and a dedicated "Support" or "Help" section. If these are missing or hard to find, your "Trust" score drops.
3. Build "Vertical" Authority
Don't try to rank for general terms like "Best ERP." Instead, create content for the specific "verticals" you serve.
 * Industry Pages: Create separate landing pages for “ERP for Manufacturing,” “ERP for Retail,” or “ERP for Human Resources.” * Comparison Tables: Since users often compare ERPs, create a page: “Prime7 ERP vs. SAP vs. Oracle: Which is right for your SMB?” This captures "high-intent" traffic.
4. Optimize for "Interaction to Next Paint" (INP)
For 2026, Google has shifted focus to how fast a site feels when a user clicks a button.
 * The "Demo" Button: Your most important interaction is likely the "Request a Demo" button. If that form takes more than 200ms to respond or load, it signals a poor user experience, which can lower your ranking.
 * Reduce Javascript: ERP websites often load heavy scripts for dashboards. Ensure these don't "block" the initial loading of your marketing site.
5. Leverage "Social Proof" (Off-Page SEO)
Google uses third-party mentions to verify if your software is real and reliable.
 * Software Directories: Get listed (and collect reviews) on sites like Capterra, G2, and SoftwareSuggest.
 * Backlink Strategy: Reach out to industry-specific publications to write guest articles about "Digital Transformation" or "Data Security in ERPs," linking back to your site.
Quick Checklist for your Developer:
 * Schema Markup: Is "SoftwareApplication" schema implemented? (This tells Google exactly what your product is).
 * Mobile Usability: Check the "Free Demo" form on a mobile device. If it's hard to type in, Google will penalize the site in mobile search results.
 * Core Web Vitals: Run a PageSpeed Insights report. If your "Performance" score is below 80, your ranking will be suppressed regardless of your content.
