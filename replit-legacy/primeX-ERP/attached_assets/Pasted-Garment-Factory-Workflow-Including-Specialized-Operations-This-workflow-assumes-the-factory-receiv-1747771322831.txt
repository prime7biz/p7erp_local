Garment Factory Workflow (Including Specialized Operations)
This workflow assumes the factory receives orders (potentially from various clients or a central merchandising unit not acting as a full "buying house" in the previous sense) and manages production, including specialized processes which might be in-house or subcontracted.

Phase 1: Order Reception & Pre-Production Planning

Order Intake & Confirmation:
Receive Order Details (Style, Quantity, Sizes, Delivery Date, Target Price if applicable).
Tech Pack & Specification Sheet Review: Analyze garment construction, materials, artwork, wash details.
Feasibility Study: Assess capacity for all required processes (Knitting, Dyeing, Printing, Embroidery, Washing, Sewing). Decide if any process needs subcontracting.
Costing & Bill of Materials (BOM):
Detailed Costing: Calculate costs for yarn, knitting, dyeing, fabric (if sourced directly), printing, embroidery, washing, trims, labor, overheads, and subcontracting.
Bill of Materials (BOM) Creation:
Yarn BOM: Yarn type, count, composition, quantity per garment/fabric unit.
Fabric BOM: Fabric type, GSM, width, consumption per garment.
Dyeing BOM: Dyes, chemicals per kg of fabric/batch.
Printing/Embroidery BOM: Inks, screens, threads, backing materials.
Trims & Accessories BOM: Labels, tags, buttons, zippers, polybags, cartons.
Sampling & Approvals:
Develop Proto, Fit, Size-Set, and Pre-Production Samples (PPS).
Lab Dip Approvals (for dyeing).
Strike-Off Approvals (for printing/embroidery).
Wash Standard Approval.
Client/Merchandiser approval of PPS is critical before bulk production.
Time & Action (T&amp;A) Calendar:
Create a detailed T&amp;A plan for all stages: Yarn procurement, knitting, dyeing, fabric procurement (if applicable), printing, embroidery, cutting, sewing, washing, finishing, packing, and dispatch.
Monitor progress against T&amp;A.
Phase 2: Material Requirement Planning (MRP) & Procurement

MRP Calculation:
Based on approved BOM and order quantity, calculate total requirements for:
Yarn (for knitting)
Greige Fabric (if knitting is outsourced or fabric bought greige)
Dyes & Chemicals (for dyeing)
Ready Fabric (if sourced directly as dyed/printed)
Printing Inks & Screens
Embroidery Threads & Backing
Trims & Accessories
Inventory Check & Procurement:
Check existing stock levels.
Raise Purchase Orders (POs) for required materials.
Supplier selection and follow-up.
Material Inward & QC:
Receive materials.
Incoming Quality Control (IQC):
Yarn: Count, strength, evenness.
Fabric: GSM, width, construction, defects, shade (if bought ready).
Dyes/Chemicals: Purity, concentration.
Trims: Quality, quantity, specifications.
Record accepted/rejected materials in ERP.
Phase 3: Production Execution & Monitoring

(For each sub-process below, if it's subcontracted, the ERP should manage Subcontract Orders, material issuance to the subcontractor, WIP tracking, and receiving processed goods with QC.)

Knitting (if applicable):
Work Order Creation: For knitting specific quantity, GSM, fabric type.
Yarn Issuance: Issue yarn from inventory to knitting machines/section.
Knitting Process: Machine operation, monitoring.
Greige Fabric QC: Check for knitting defects, GSM, width.
Update Greige Fabric Inventory.
Dyeing (if applicable):
Work Order Creation: For dyeing specific batches (color, fabric, quantity).
Greige Fabric/Yarn Issuance: Issue fabric/yarn to dyeing house/machines.
Dyeing Process: Recipe adherence, machine operation.
Dyed Fabric/Yarn QC: Shade matching against approved lab dip, colorfastness, shrinkage.
Update Dyed Fabric/Yarn Inventory.
All Over Printing (AOP) / Fabric Printing (if applicable, often before cutting):
Work Order Creation: For printing specific design, fabric quantity.
Fabric (Greige/Dyed) Issuance: Issue fabric to printing section/subcontractor.
Printing Process: Screen preparation/digital printing, machine operation.
Printed Fabric QC: Print quality, registration, color matching against strike-off.
Update Printed Fabric Inventory.
Cutting:
Work Order Creation: For cutting specific styles, quantities, sizes.
Fabric Issuance: Issue dyed/printed/finished fabric from inventory.
Spreading, Marker Making, Cutting.
Cut Panel QC: Check for defects, accurate cutting.
Bundling & Numbering: Track cut parts.
Value Addition on Cut Panels (Pre-Sewing):
Embroidery (if applicable):
Work Order Creation.
Cut Panel Issuance to embroidery section/subcontractor.
Embroidery Process.
Embroidered Panel QC.
Placement Print (if applicable):
Work Order Creation.
Cut Panel Issuance to printing section/subcontractor.
Placement Printing Process.
Printed Panel QC.
Sewing:
Work Order Creation: For assembling garments.
Bundled Cut Panel (+ Printed/Embroidered Panels) Issuance to sewing lines.
Trims Issuance to lines.
Sewing Process: Assembly according to spec sheet.
Inline QC: Checkpoints during sewing to catch defects early.
End-of-Line QC.
Washing (Garment Washing, if applicable):
Work Order Creation: For specific wash type, quantity.
Sewn Garment Issuance to washing section/subcontractor.
Washing Process: Recipe adherence, machine operation.
Washed Garment QC: Hand feel, appearance, shrinkage, shade against wash standard.
Finishing:
Thread Trimming, Initial Checking.
Buttoning, Riveting, Label Attachment (if not done in sewing).
Pressing/Ironing.
Final Quality Check (FQC): Measurement checks, workmanship, overall appearance based on AQL standards.
Record FQC results.
Phase 4: Packing & Dispatch

Packing:
Folding, tagging, poly-bagging as per client requirements.
Carton packing, marking, and labeling.
Internal Final Audit:
Random audit of packed goods before dispatch.
Dispatch Planning:
Generate packing lists, delivery challans.
Goods Dispatch:
Update inventory (Finished Goods).
Shipment tracking (if ERP supports).
Phase 5: Subcontractor Management (if applicable for any process)

Subcontractor Master Data.
Subcontract Order (SCO) Creation: Specify process, quantity, rates, delivery dates.
Material Issuance to Subcontractor: Track materials sent out.
Work-in-Progress (WIP) Tracking with subcontractor.
Receiving Processed Goods:
Quantity reconciliation.
Quality Check of Subcontracted Goods.
Subcontractor Bill Booking & Payment.

Business Logic (High-Level Examples)
BOM & Costing:
Logic: When an order style is selected, the system retrieves its master BOM. For each BOM item (yarn, fabric, dye, trim), it calculates required quantity based on order quantity and consumption rate. Costs are fetched from material master/last purchase price/standard cost. For processes (knitting, dyeing, sewing, etc.), standard minute values (SMVs) or process costs are applied.
Subcontracting Cost: If a process is marked for subcontracting, the system should fetch rates from the subcontractor master or allow manual entry.
MRP Calculation:
Logic: Net Requirement (Material X) = (Total Order Qty * BOM Qty of X per Garment) - Current Stock of X - Expected Incoming Stock of X. This runs for all BOM components.
For Intermediates (e.g., Greige Fabric for Dyeing): Greige Fabric Needed = Total Dyed Fabric Required for Order. Then, Yarn for Greige = Greige Fabric Needed * Yarn Consumption per unit of Greige.
Work Order Generation (e.g., for Dyeing):
Logic: If Order Requires Dyed Fabric Y and PPS Approved and Greige Fabric Available/Planned, then Create Dyeing Work Order for X kg of Fabric Type Z to Shade A. Assign to In-house Dyeing Dept or Subcontractor B. Link to original Sales Order.
Subcontracting Process Flow:
Logic: IF WorkOrder.Process_Location = 'Subcontractor'
THEN Create SubcontractOrder (SCO) linked to WorkOrder and SalesOrder.
MaterialTransferModule.IssueMaterial (Material_List, Quantity_List, SCO_ID, Subcontractor_ID).
WIP_Module.Track_SCO_Status (SCO_ID).
Upon Receipt from Subcontractor: QualityCheckModule.InspectReceived (SCO_ID, Received_Qty).
IF QC_Pass THEN InventoryModule.UpdateStock (Processed_Item, Received_Qty) ELSE Handle_Rejection().
FinanceModule.Process_Subcontractor_Bill (SCO_ID, Approved_Qty).
Quality Control Trigger:
Logic: AFTER ProductionStage_Completion (e.g., Knitting_Batch_End, Dyeing_Batch_End, Cutting_Lot_End, Sewing_Line_Output)
THEN Trigger_QC_Check (Stage_Name, Batch_ID/Lot_ID, QC_Parameters_From_SpecSheet).
Record_QC_Results (Defects, Measurements, Pass/Fail).
IF Fail AND Critical_Defect THEN Hold_Batch AND Notify_Production_Manager.
Database Relationship Suggestions (Conceptual for PostgreSQL)
Core Tables:

Tenants (tenant_id PK, name)
Users (user_id PK, tenant_id FK, username, role)
Clients (client_id PK, tenant_id FK, name, details)
Orders (order_id PK, tenant_id FK, client_id FK, order_date, delivery_date, status)
Products (product_id PK, tenant_id FK, style_code, description, image_url) (Represents the final garment style)
Order_Line_Items (order_line_item_id PK, order_id FK, product_id FK, color, size, quantity, unit_price)
Tech_Packs (tech_pack_id PK, product_id FK, version, spec_details JSONB)
Materials (material_id PK, tenant_id FK, material_code, name, type TEXT, unit_of_measure, specs JSONB) (Types: YARN, FABRIC_GREIGE, FABRIC_DYED, FABRIC_PRINTED, DYE, CHEMICAL, TRIM, ACCESSORY)
BOM_Headers (bom_id PK, product_id FK, version, created_date)
BOM_Lines (bom_line_id PK, bom_id FK, material_id FK, consumption_rate, notes)
Suppliers (supplier_id PK, tenant_id FK, name, type TEXT) (Type: Material Supplier, Subcontractor)
Production & Operations Tables:

Production_Orders (prod_order_id PK, tenant_id FK, order_line_item_id FK, product_id FK, quantity, status, due_date, type TEXT) (Type: KNITTING, DYEING, CUTTING, SEWING, AOP, EMBROIDERY, WASHING, FINISHING)
Production_Order_Materials (prod_order_material_id PK, prod_order_id FK, material_id FK, required_qty, issued_qty)
Production_Order_Steps (prod_order_step_id PK, prod_order_id FK, step_name TEXT, machine_id FK, employee_id FK, start_time, end_time, output_qty, qc_status)
Machines (machine_id PK, tenant_id FK, name, type TEXT, capacity, department_id FK) (Type: Knitting Machine, Dyeing Machine, Cutting Table, Sewing Machine, Printing Machine, Embroidery Machine, Washing Machine)
Departments (department_id PK, tenant_id FK, name) (e.g., Knitting, Dyeing, Cutting, Sewing Line A, Printing, Embroidery, Washing, Finishing, Stores)
Inventory (inventory_id PK, tenant_id FK, material_id FK, batch_no, location_id FK, quantity_on_hand, last_updated)
Inventory_Transactions (transaction_id PK, tenant_id FK, material_id FK, transaction_type TEXT, quantity, transaction_date, reference_id, reference_type TEXT) (Ref Types: PO, ProdOrder, SCO, Adjustment)
Specialized Operations & Subcontracting:

Knitting_Orders (knitting_order_id PK REFERENCES Production_Orders(prod_order_id), greige_fabric_specs JSONB, target_gsm, target_width)
Dyeing_Orders (dyeing_order_id PK REFERENCES Production_Orders(prod_order_id), fabric_type_id FK REFERENCES Materials(material_id), color_code, recipe_id FK, batch_size_kg)
Dyeing_Recipes (recipe_id PK, tenant_id FK, color_code, fabric_type_id FK, steps JSONB) (Steps include dyes, chemicals, temperature, time)
Printing_Orders (printing_order_id PK REFERENCES Production_Orders(prod_order_id), print_type TEXT, design_artwork_id FK, ink_specs JSONB) (Type: AOP, PLACEMENT)
Embroidery_Orders (embroidery_order_id PK REFERENCES Production_Orders(prod_order_id), design_file_id FK, thread_specs JSONB)
Washing_Orders (washing_order_id PK REFERENCES Production_Orders(prod_order_id), wash_type_id FK, recipe_id FK)
Subcontract_Orders (sco_id PK, tenant_id FK, supplier_id FK REFERENCES Suppliers(supplier_id), prod_order_id FK REFERENCES Production_Orders(prod_order_id), process_type TEXT, order_date, expected_delivery_date, status)
Subcontract_Order_Items (sco_item_id PK, sco_id FK, material_id FK (item being processed), quantity, rate)
Subcontract_Material_Issuance (sco_issue_id PK, sco_id FK, material_id FK (raw material issued), issued_qty, issue_date)
Subcontract_Receipts (sco_receipt_id PK, sco_id FK, received_qty, receipt_date, qc_status)
Quality Control:

QC_Templates (qc_template_id PK, tenant_id FK, process_name TEXT, parameters JSONB)
QC_Checks (qc_check_id PK, tenant_id FK, reference_id, reference_type TEXT, qc_template_id FK, check_date, inspector_id FK, status, results JSONB) (Ref Type: PO_INWARD, PROD_ORDER_KNITTING, PROD_ORDER_DYEING, etc.)
Relationships (Examples):

Orders 1:M Order_Line_Items
Order_Line_Items 1:M Production_Orders (potentially, or an order could have multiple production orders for different components/processes)
Products 1:1 (or 1:M if versioned) BOM_Headers
BOM_Headers 1:M BOM_Lines
BOM_Lines M:1 Materials
Production_Orders M:1 Departments
Production_Orders 1:M Production_Order_Steps
Production_Orders 1:1 (or 1:M if stages) Subcontract_Orders (if that production order or a part of it is subcontracted)
Suppliers (type='Subcontractor') 1:M Subcontract_Orders
QC_Checks M:1 Production_Orders (or Inventory_Transactions for IQC, Subcontract_Receipts for SC QC)
This structure allows tracking materials and processes from order to dispatch, including specialized in-house or outsourced operations, within a multi-tenant environment. You'd use tenant_id in almost every table to ensure data segregation. JSONB fields in PostgreSQL are useful for flexible specifications like tech pack details, QC parameters, or recipe steps.
