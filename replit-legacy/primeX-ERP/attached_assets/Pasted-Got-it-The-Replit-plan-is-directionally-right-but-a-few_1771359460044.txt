Got it. The Replit plan is directionally right, but a few items will cause accounting + stock control problems if you build it exactly like that—especially the part where Delivery Challan approval auto-creates a receivable bill reference.

Here’s my revised plan (Tally-aligned + ERP-safe), with key fixes and a clearer build order.

What I would fix in the Replit plan (critical)
1) Do NOT create bill reference from Delivery Challan

Delivery Challan / Gate Pass = goods movement

Invoice / Purchase Bill = financial liability/receivable
In Tally, bill-wise outstanding is driven by Sales Invoice / Purchase Bill, not by Delivery Note approval.

✅ Correct rule:

Challan posted → Stock OUT

Invoice posted → AR bill reference (receivable)

Purchase Bill posted → AP bill reference (payable)

You can allow “Invoice from Challan” (1-click) to keep it fast.

2) “On Account” vs “Advance” must be consistent

In practical accounting:

Advance = party-wise credit balance (unadjusted) that can later be linked to invoices.

On Account = payment/receipt posted without bill allocation (still affects party ledger), but won’t reduce specific bill outstanding.

✅ Best implementation:

Keep both, but treat them as two allocation types in the bill allocation engine.

3) Party system should come earlier than Cost Center + Bill-wise UI

Because:

Bill-wise is always “per party”

Orders, challans, gate passes, invoices all require Party

✅ Put Party earlier.

4) Workflow stages are fine, but align “posting” semantics

Workflow can be:
DRAFT → SUBMITTED → CHECKED → RECOMMENDED → APPROVED → POSTED
But only POSTED should hit:

stock ledger

accounting ledger

bill references
